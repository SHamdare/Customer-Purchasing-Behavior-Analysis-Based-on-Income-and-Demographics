# 📊 Customer Purchasing Behavior Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior using marketing data to understand how income and demographic factors influence spending patterns. The goal is to uncover insights that can support data-driven marketing strategies and customer segmentation.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Explore the relationship between income and customer spending  
- Analyze how demographic factors (age, education, etc.) affect purchasing behavior  
- Identify patterns that can support targeted marketing decisions  
- Visualize insights using data visualization techniques  

---

## 📂 Dataset
- File: `marketing_data.csv`  
- The dataset includes:
  - Customer income  
  - Demographic information (age, education, marital status)  
  - Spending and purchasing behavior  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Seaborn  
- Matplotlib  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 🔍 Data Preparation
- Cleaned column names for consistency  
- Handled missing values where necessary  
- Converted data types for analysis  
- Filtered numerical variables for correlation analysis  

---

## 📊 Analysis & Visualisations

The project includes:
- Distribution of customer income (boxplots & histograms)  
- Relationship between income and spending (scatter plots)  
- Spending patterns across demographic groups (bar plots & boxplots)  
- Correlation heatmap of numerical variables  

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- Higher income customers tend to spend more, indicating a positive relationship between income and purchasing behavior  
- Certain demographic groups (based on age and education) show distinct spending patterns  
- Some variables show strong correlations, suggesting potential drivers of customer behavior  
- Customer segmentation opportunities exist based on income and demographics  

---

## 📈 Correlation Analysis
Correlation analysis was performed using only numerical variables, as categorical variables cannot be directly included without encoding. The heatmap highlights relationships between income, spending, and other numerical features.

---

## ⚠️ Limitations
- Categorical variables were not fully encoded for advanced modeling  
- The dataset may not capture all external factors influencing purchasing behavior  
- Correlation does not imply causation  

---

## ✅ Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates that income and demographic characteristics play a significant role in customer purchasing behavior. The findings can help businesses better understand their customers and design targeted marketing strategies to improve engagement and sales.
